FAQs — clear answers, no jargon.

A growing reference for clients and curious builders. Click a question to expand.

A wireframe is a low-fidelity layout using boxes that resemble wires. The intent is to map out the site structure like flow—navigation, sections, and define priorities—without final colors or imagery. It lets us validate the UX quickly before further design and development commitment. This is just a building block for clarity and the developers understanding, and not a final draft.

A hi-fidelity design that shows the final look and feel plus supporting documentation—accepted requirements, UI rules, and notes we both use as the reference going into the build.

Clear, testable statements that define “done.” They keep scope aligned and make sign-off objective for both of us.

QA (quality assurance) verifies behavior across devices and browsers—links, forms, performance, accessibility basics, and edge cases—before launch.

A repository (“repo”) is the code home (e.g., GitHub). We create it cleanly and hand it off—under your account— so you retain full control and can work with any developer in the future.

Staging is a private preview environment for reviews and QA. Production is the live site your customers see. We deploy to staging first, then promote to production once approved.

Your domain is the address (example.com). Hosting is the server where the site runs. SSL adds encryption (the lock icon) for security and trust—important for SEO and user confidence.

A responsive site adapts to screens of all sizes—phone, tablet, desktop—so content remains usable and consistent without separate mobile versions.

Semantic HTML, clean URLs, meta tags, performance and accessibility fundamentals, and structured content patterns that search engines can parse—all baked in from the start.

Yes—Google Analytics or alternatives (e.g., Plausible, Umami). You own the accounts; we configure tracking and a simple dashboard so you can monitor traffic and trends.

A local maximum is a “good enough” solution that prevents you from seeing a better one. We step back, survey the landscape, and design paths that aim for the higher peak—long-term impact over quick wins.

Feedback is built into each phase with scheduled review meetings and dedicated dev time. Additional iterations are available hourly—clear, transparent, and paced to your goals.

Yes—keyboard navigation, color contrast, alt text, and semantic structure are part of our baseline. Accessibility is good practice and good business.

If you want a CMS, we’ll recommend a right-sized option (from lightweight markdown to headless platforms) so you can update content without touching code.

You do. Domains, hosting, email, fonts, and any paid services run under your accounts. We build for handoff so you’re never locked in.

Timelines depend on scope and content readiness. Typical ranges: single-page sites ~1–2 weeks after mockup; multi-page builds ~3–5+ weeks. We’ll confirm a schedule with your proposal.

Complexity (features, integrations), content needs, and timeline. Packages give a starting point; extras like premium plugins or third-party services are billed directly to your accounts.
